Introduction

Tra Vinh's boulevards, one of the most beautiful towns in the Mekong Delta, are still lined with shade trees, harkening back to a bygone age. Tra Vinh is a peaceful spot to explore the Mekong's little-known Cambodian link, with more than 140 Khmer pagodas dotting the province. Now that big bridges connect it to Ben Tre and beyond, the town itself sees a little more tourist traffic. Tra Vinh province is home to around 300,000 ethnic Khmer. They may appear to be an inconspicuous minority since they all speak fluent Vietnamese and have no distinguishing dress or way of life. If you look a little closer, you'll find that Khmer culture is still alive and strong in certain regions of Vietnam. In Tra Vinh, there is also a tiny but active Chinese community, one of the few remaining in the Mekong Delta region.

Weather and climate in Tra Vinh

The weather is pleasant in January. The temperature climbs to 30°C, with 12mm of rain expected every month.
The weather is pleasant during the months of February and March. The temperature averages 28°C by early evening, and March sees approximately 6mm of rain.
From April through May, the weather is ideal. On average, it is 29°C in the mornings, and it rains around 58mm each month.
The weather is quite adverse from June to September. In September, the highest temperature is 31°C.
In October, the weather is ideal. The temperature reaches 31°C, and around 90mm of rain falls in October.
The climate is pleasant from November through December. The temperature rises to 26°C by early evening.
